The Unleasing of Harish Bhat Pakshi Vana: A historical start of ecological hub

In the month of March, 2017, the pond was excavated at the bird park and the soil necessary for planting the saplings were brought in. Muthappanna, who was taking care of the nursery had started procuring the saplings from the list of plants given by Harish R Bhat. By May, 2017, the soil brought in was laid out and ready for planting. As soon as the rain started by mid-May, buffalo grass were planted across to stabilize the soil..

We have the honour of seeing the peaceful coexistence of neighbourhood birds and their natural habitat in our Harish Bhatt Pakshivana. Our park attracts a variety of native bird species because of the plentiful food sources and safe haven we offer. Visitors can therefore delight in the charming sight of these feathered creatures gracefully soaring through the air and landing to eat the food we provide.

By luring local birds, we support a healthy ecosystem and aid in the preservation of the biodiversity of the area. We recognise the importance of supplying these birds with a steady supply of food because doing so not only promotes their welfare but also encourages their continued presence in the park. In order to prioritise the birds' nutritional needs and reduce any potential negative effects on their natural behaviour and health, we have implemented responsible feeding practises.
