Harish Bhat Pakshi Vana:

Welcome to the Harish Bhat Pakshi Vana, nestled within the vibrant campus, is a sanctuary for nature enthusiasts and a haven for birds of various species. At this unique bird paradise, we aim to create an immersive experience where visitors can appreciate the beauty of our feathered friends in a natural unconfined environment. With lush greenery, tranquil ponds, and carefully designed habitats, we strive to provide a nurturing space for both resident and migratory birds to thrive.

We have the honour of seeing the peaceful coexistence of neighbourhood birds and their natural habitat in our Harish Bhatt Pakshivana. Our park attracts a variety of native bird species because of the plentiful food sources and safe haven we offer. Visitors can therefore delight in the charming sight of these feathered creatures gracefully soaring through the air and landing to eat the food we provide.

By luring local birds, we support a healthy ecosystem and aid in the preservation of the biodiversity of the area. We recognise the importance of supplying these birds with a steady supply of food because doing so not only promotes their welfare but also encourages their continued presence in the park. In order to prioritise the birds' nutritional needs and reduce any potential negative effects on their natural behaviour and health, we have implemented responsible feeding practises.

Our Vision

" Our Vision for the bird park is to plant an abundance of native beneficial trees, shrubs, wild flowers, berries, etc. in order to create a landscape that can support a rich and diversified habitat for birds. We'll also concentrate on native plants that attract advantageous insects and serve as hosts for butterflies' larvae, which are natural sources of food for insectivores. Ponds on the property will be crucial for maintaining life and will make an excellent habitat for waterflow rehabilitation and release. "

Our Mission

Mohan Alva

"Mohan Alva built the Birds Park in Alva's Education Foundation to create a space where students can learn about birds and their importance to the environment. The park is also home to over 100 species of birds and provides a sanctuary for birds that are injured or displaced. It is open to the public and is a popular destination for birdwatchers and nature lovers. The park also helps to increase awareness of bird conservation and provides a place for people to learn about birds and their importance to the environment. "
